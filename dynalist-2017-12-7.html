    • !(2017-11-02 13:00)
      Please remember when choosing a difficult emotion such as anxious, angry etc, to choose a memory where this comes up that is not too strong. So, if you were to scale the intensity of feeling this difficult emotion (10 being strongest ever,) that you would choose a memory where this emotion was around a 3 or 4 in intensity. (enough where you can feel it in your body.) When thinking of a the opposite emotion, it's OK to choose a memory that has an intensity at an 8, 9 or 10 as that supports the practice of getting "unstuck."

      Please also remember to turn to your inner resource if you feel an emotional response to be too strong to handle. Also, if another emotion comes up that seems to be stronger and "wants" your attention more than the one you originally chose to inquire into, feel free to switch to that emotion. Remembering, that this is your practice. So, if you find it to be too overwhelming all together, feel free to turn off the recording and turn on the Inner Resource, body or breath-sensing.

    • !(2017-11-09 13:00)
      Today, we discussed choosing an emotion that you want to experience more or a difficult emotion that you want to be able to tolerate more. While in a relaxed state, we imagined what it would be like to relate to our emotions as though they were alive, coming to us and having a conversation. First, describing to ourselves, what they would look like, shape, color, “flavor,” or a vague, formless presence. In choosing one particular emotion in advance (or just meeting one that arises) we engage in a conversation with them, asking them “what do you want?” “What do you need?” and what action do you want me to take into my daily life?”

      Engaging in this exercise while in a meditative state can be helpful in cultivating a relationship with our emotions while also experiencing the information they bring us. As you continue to experiment with this way of relating to emotions, please remember that you can to turn to your inner resource or Heartfelt Desire at any time, during the practice.

    • !(2017-11-16)
      We stayed with the emotion theme, adding a section that focuses on gaining larger perspective. It's called "Stepping In and Back." We discussed how stepping in and really feeling the emotion, allows us to remain connected to our body experience, and also to tolerate that experience. We also recognize when there are times when we feel overwhelmed with things, it can feel like 100% of our entire experience. But, when shifting language from, (for example,) I'm anxious" to "feelings of anxiety are present," we create a sense of distance, allowing us to experience that we are more than our emotions.

      We also take a stance of observing, helping us to access larger perspective thinking, which can lead to insight. Taking the stance of the observer, we then take another step back, toward, being the Awareness in which all of these experiences arise, and ask what that feels like. From this stance, we can tap into the general sense of well-being, that is constant, even amidst the changing circumstances that life brings our way..
      (Please also remember that you can call up your Inner Resource at any time during the meditation.)

      Research shows the physiologic benefits of experiencing joy (or any of the related emotions along the continuum of joy.) These experiences release endorphins (natural pain killers) and other chemicals that contribute to a feeling of well-being. Even small amounts of joy are shown to improve immune, respiratory and cardio-vascular functioning.

      iRest also teaches that while these moments of joy can be fleeting, practicing meditation can also lead us to accessing the Joy of Awareness, that is always present amidst the turbulence that life can bring our way.
